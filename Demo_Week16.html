What is ρ (exit density)?
The average number of agents within 90 world-units of any exit, divided by the number of exits. A value of 15 means ~15 agents are pressing toward each exit simultaneously — indicating a bottleneck. High ρ correlates with longer evacuation time and higher collision rate. This is the primary metric for comparing HSM vs SFM exit-pressure behaviour.

Behavioral Model
HSM — Hybrid Steering: Directly computes agent velocity each frame. Goal direction is primary; separation and obstacle corrections are added and clamped so goal always dominates. Congestion weight ω (set by τ) grows with local density, increasing separation influence in packed areas. Smoother flow, less oscillation near exits. (Ye et al. 2024; manuscript §VII-B)
SFM — Social Force: Agents experience forces summed to produce acceleration, then Euler-integrated to velocity. Inter-agent repulsion uses exponential decay A·exp((r−dpred)/B) with collision-predictive lookahead — predicted future distance rather than instantaneous distance. Produces stronger bottlenecks in dense regimes. (Ding et al. 2024; Wu et al. 2022; manuscript §VII-A)
